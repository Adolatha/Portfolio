Threat Intelligence: From Threats to Tactics

The event “Threat Intelligence: From Threats to Tactics” provided valuable insight into how the Centre for Cybersecurity Belgium (CCB) applies cyber threat intelligence in practice to protect organisations and citizens across Belgium.

During the session, Sandro Manzo, Lead of the Fusion Centre at CCB/CyTRIS, and Niels Desloover, Cyber Threat Intelligence Analyst at CCB/CyTRIS, explained how threat intelligence is operationalised within Belgium’s national cybersecurity ecosystem. Rather than treating intelligence as simply a collection of threat reports, their work focuses on translating threat data into concrete defensive actions that help organisations anticipate and mitigate attacks.

One of the most interesting aspects discussed during the talk was how CCB actively identifies emerging threats and communicates them to potential victims. When analysts detect suspicious activity or ongoing malicious campaigns targeting Belgian organisations, the objective is not only to investigate the threat but also to warn the organisations that may be affected.

This early warning approach allows organisations to strengthen their defences before attackers are able to successfully compromise systems.

The speakers also introduced several initiatives that support this approach driven by intelligence. One example is the Spear Warning system, which helps identify and track spear-phishing campaigns targeting organisations.

Another initiative discussed was the Red Button project, which provides a mechanism for reporting suspicious emails and online threats. Reports submitted through this channel contribute to a larger pool of threat intelligence that analysts can use to identify patterns, investigate campaigns, and improve situational awareness.

Reflecting on the event, it became clear that cyber threat intelligence is most effective when it moves beyond observation and becomes part of active defence. Intelligence analysis, early detection, and coordinated communication can significantly strengthen national cyber resilience.
